Concerts

Manhattan is well known for its concerts in Madison Square Garden as well as its multiple Broadway shows. However, many restaurants in the city feature musical artists playing either music or covers of other famous songs. Several city parks also have options to see concerts for free. Like the park movies, they’re most popular on summer weekends and also cover a wide genre. In the subway station it is possible to hear people playing music as well.


Movies

Aside from New York City’s numerous movie theaters, there are sometimes free movies shown in parks. Mostly playing on summer weekends, they are broadcasted on parks or the Hudson River after dark. They range from old classics to modern blockbusters to indie flicks and have options for every age group. Moviegoers can bring their own food or buy food at nearby food trucks.


Art

Multiple sculptures and showcases of art dot New York City. Several of the more famous sculptures are the Charging Bull in downtown Manhattan and Atlas by the Rockefeller Center. Central Park also houses multiple statues of either famous people in history or other people and animals. Similarly, public murals and other artworks are scattered across the buildings. Parks and Beaches

Parks and Beaches While the most famous park is Central Park, there are over 1700 parks scattered across the city. They range from large areas of land with trees and picnic space to smaller playgrounds. Staten Island has the most parks and green areas of all the boroughs. Similarly, there are also beaches in all the boroughs except Manhattan. The most famous of them is Brooklyn’s Coney Island, which is also home to an amusement park.


Staten Island Ferry

Staten Island is the only borough to rely on just one train line, as it uses more bus lines. Instead of driving over the Verrazano Bridge to get there, people prefer taking the Staten Island Ferry. The boat ride is about twenty minutes long and it’s free, but does not carry cars. The boat also passes by the Statue of Liberty on its journey. The Manhattan terminal is in Battery Park in the downtown area of Manhattan.


Gold Vault Tour and Museum

The Gold Vault is located in the Federal Reserve Bank. It is home to the largest collection of gold bars in the world, each of which weigh 28 pounds. The bank currently holds about 10 percent of the world’s gold. While it doesn’t owe the gold and instead holds it, they do allow people to tour that area and educate tourists about the Federal Reserve system.


Yoga and Tai Chi

In many public parks, outside activities like yoga or tai chi are very popular. Outside classes are free, though several gyms feature them as well. Both serve as calming breaks for the practitioners in the morning or early evening. Each borough has several parks that feature either one or both of them.


Street Fairs and Festivals

Every summer, New York City boasts many street fairs and parades. Food festivals like San Gennaro Feast and Brooklyn’s Smorgasburg, sell food from different vendors that come from different cultures. Other street fairs sell clothing and jewelry from different places. There are also multiple parades year round that celebrate people’s heritages which sell food as well. While most parades are during the summer, the most well known ones are the Thanksgiving and Christmas parades.
